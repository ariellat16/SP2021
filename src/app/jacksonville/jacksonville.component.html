JACKSONVILLE MUST GO PLACES

7. Guana Beach


The Guana Reserve Beach is a favorite among Ponte Vedra residents. After paying a parking fee of three dollars per vehicle, visitors can go to the beach, enjoy beautiful nature trails at the Deep Creek State Forest, go canoeing, and many other activities. The GTM Research Reserve Visitor Center, Beach Access Parking Lots, and Trails can be found on A1A, south of Jacksonville and north of St. Augustine.

6. Big Talbot Island State Park


Located on State Road A1A North Jacksonville, Fl, Big Talbot Island State Park is open from 8 a.m. to sundown every day of the year. Individuals can enjoy boating, fishing, hiking, paddling, biking, and may make a reservation to camp on the grounds.

5. Five Points


Five Points is loved by Jacksonville residents for its history, culture, art, entertainment, dining, and shops. This bohemian neighborhood is known for its delicious restaurants, coffee and dessert cafes, vintage stores, and is home of the Sun-Ray Cinema.

4. Riverside Arts Market


Open Saturdays from 10 to 3, the Riverside Arts Market is a local farmers market that is popular for its local produce, art, jewelry, coffee, and baked goods. It is located in Downtown Jacksonville under the bridge. If lucky enough, there may be live yoga at 9 am, check out their facebook page to see the dates!

3. Cummer Museum of Art and Gardens


Located in Jacksonville’s historic Riverside, the Cummer Museum of Art & Gardens is loved by individuals of all ages. Established in 1958, the Museum now holds over 5,000 works of art, is home to multiple historic gardens, and educates visitors daily.

2. Downtown St. Augustine


With buildings from the 1700s, the St. Augustine Historic District is home to hotels, attractions, popular restaurants, and shops. Guests enjoy walking down St. George Street, visiting the Castillo De San Marcos, and touring the St. Augustine Lighthouse and Maritime Museum.

1. St. Johns Town Center


Many would call the St. Johns Town Center Jacksonville’s most popular location. With over 175 stores including Apple, Nordstrom, Tesla, and Dillards, the mall also has many upscale dining options.
